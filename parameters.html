All Reviewed Clinical Decision SUpport Systems:

NAVIFY Mutation Profiler

Made by Roche

NAVIFY Mutation Profiler is CE-IVD-certified software that helps interpret clinically important, detected variants, e.g., disease-associated variant instances, broader variant groups, variant combinations, and variants of unknown significance, acquired by NGS testing.

webpage

WATSON FOR ONCOLOGY

Made by IBM

Watson for Oncology (WFO) looks for traits in patients’ EHRs, genetic information, and operability status to find suitable treatment plans. It then uses machine learning and natural language processing techniques to process text documents and identify literature matching the patients’ characteristics. As an output, WFO produces ranked recommendations, such as anticancer drugs, their combinations, and radiation and hormone therapies, with the associated NCCN guidelines and other evidence to support the recommendations.

webpage

QIAGEN Clinical Insight Interpret One

Made by QIAGEN Digital Insights

QIAGEN Clinical Insights is a commercial clinical decision support tool portfolio that includes tools that translates molecular data into therapy recommendations.

webpage

Oncology Pharmacotherapy Decision Support System (OncoPDSS)

Made by the Cancer Precision Medicine group of the Bioinformatics Research Institute of National Enineering Research Center for Beijing Biochip Technology, China

Oncology Pharmacotherapy Decision Support System (OncoPDSS) is a web server that can be used by researchers and clinicians alike, as it serves as a knowledge base on cancer precision medicine. The evidence-based CDSS helps clinicians with pharmacotherapy decision-making by using actionable alterations associated with clinical implications to interpret sequencing results accurately at the individual level.

webpage

CureMatch

Made by CureMatch, Inc.

CureMatch Bionov is a HIPAA and GDPR-compliant web-based CDSS platform focused on personalized medicine, which provides oncological combination therapies based on entire molecular profiles. Cure- Match also considers other patient characteristics besides molecular data, for example, comorbidities, medical history, or previous treatment lines.

webpage

OncoKB

Made by Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center

Developed at the Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center, OncoKB is a precision oncology knowledge base. It comprises biological and clinical data regarding the genetic mutations that occur in cancer.

webpage

Cancer Genome Interpreter

Made by Barcelona Biomedical Genomics Lab

Cancer Genome Interpreter is another platform for interpreting tumor alterations according to their biological and clinical relevance. The system supports clinical decision-making by annotating potential tumorigenic alterations and their effect on treatment response in newly sequenced cancer genomes based on various evidence levels.

webpage

Prostate Cancer Survival Calculator According to Initial Treatment Modality

Made by The Department of Urology, Yonsei University College of Medicine & Selvas AI, Seoul

Prostate Cancer Survival Calculator According to Initial Treatment Modality employs an artificial neural network model to predict survival outcomes according to 5 routine initial treatment modalities: active surveillance, radical prostatectomy, radiation therapy with androgen deprivation therapy, radiation therapy without androgen deprivation therapy, and androgen deprivation therapy.

webpage

ProRaDS

Made by The D-Lab, Department of Precision Medicine, GROW—School for Oncology and Developmental Biology, Maastricht University

This relatively new CDSS developed in the Netherlands is focused on helping oncologists select a patient-specific treatment from two standard therapies—radical prostatectomy (RP) and external beam radiotherapy (EBRT)—for low- to intermediate-risk patients diagnosed with PC. The CDSS achieves this by calculating the quality-adjusted life years (QALYs) after 20 years and the probability percentages of the following outcomes for RP and EBRT treatments: erectile dysfunction at two years, rectal bleeding at three years, urinary incontinence at one year, and cancer free at five years.

webpage

The DESIREE Project

Made by Vicomtech (Spain), Arivis AG (Germany), Assistance Publique – Hôpitaux de Paris (France), Bilbomatica (Spain), ERESA (Spain), Fundación Onkologikoa (Spain), INSERM-LIMICS (France), Medical Innovation and Technology (Greece), Sistemas Genómicos (Spain), Houston Methodist Research Institute (USA) & Ulster University (UK)

The DESIREE Project, funded by The European Union, started in 2016 and was completed in July 2019. One of Desiree’s many goals was to develop several tools focused on handling primary breast cancer. They designed and validated a decision support tool for the DESIREE platform to deliver three types of recommendations: guideline-based, case-based, and experience-based.

webpage

Predict: Breast Cancer

Made by The Winton Centre for Risk & Evidence Communication

Predict: Breast Cancer consists of prediction models that can be used to help make informed decisions about treatment following surgery for early invasive breast cancer. Based on several patient parameters, it calculates the overall survival rates after five, ten, and fifteen years based on the selected post-surgery treatment plans. The treatment options included in the predictions are hormone therapy, chemotherapy (second and third generation), trastuzumab, bisphosphonates, and combinations of the forenamed therapies.

webpage

corRECTreatment

Made by Department of Medical Informatics - Dokuz Eylül University and Department of Biostatistics and Medical informatics - Ege University

CorRECTreatment is an online CDS tool that serves to aid physicians in the selection of the most appropriate treatment methods for rectal cancer patients. Additionally, the tool can be used to predict an average overall survival time for the suggested treatment methods.
