CorRECTreatment

webpage link

CorRECTreatment is an online CDS tool that serves to aid physicians in the selection of the most appropriate treatment methods for rectal cancer patients. Additionally, the tool can be used to predict an average overall survival time for the suggested treatment methods [1]. The CDSS is available at https://medinfo.deu.edu.tr/dsrct/.

Approach
Suner et al. [1] selected a combination of two approaches to create a decision model for patient-specific treatment options, the analytical hierarchy process (AHP) method and a decision tree model.

The AHP method was used to mimic the natural decision-making process of clinicians. From 388 patient data records from 1988 to 2010, the criteria that affect decision-making, criteria levels, and priorities were calculated via the AHP method. For instance, in the first step, the criterion with the highest priority is "the presence of perforation", and in the second step, "resection margin status" has the highest priority [1].

Subsequently, the calculated priorities and recommended treatment options according to the criteria levels were used to form a decision tree. Moreover, the probabilities of recommended treatments were assigned, and the average overall survival values of the treatments were calculated from patient data [1].

Usage
The CDSS works in two steps. The first step suggests treatment methods based on eight input parameters (criteria) provided by the user. Afterward, additional treatments to the recommended treatment and the predicted average survival time are offered as the output of the second step. This step uses ten criteria. In both steps, the recommendations are sorted from the highest probable method to be recommended to the lowest [1].

Validation
Two general surgeons were involved in the validation of the CDSS. The experts provided their insights on the best treatment methods and average overall survival values for scenarios derived from 20 cases, ten common and ten rare, from the patient dataset. Suner et al. [1] then examined the consistency of compatibility of expert opinions and the DSSs outputs. For the first step, the consistency values for common and rare cases were 80% and 50%, respectively. Likewise, in the second step, the values were 100% and 80%. This level of consistency indicates that the model performs well [1].

Limitations
The current decision tree model contains unusable branches due to the lack of corresponding patient data; thus, some treatment methods cannot be recommended. If there is no patient in their database comprising 388 records that meets the selection criteria, the CDSS will not provide any recommendations [59]. However, the structure of the database does allow the incorporation of new cases so that the model could use the unusable branches to recommend other treatment options in future versions of the system [1].

Moreover, the patient data on which the system is based was obtained from a single facility and is quite outdated. Current patient data from other institutions could improve the tool by making it more general. The system has also not been evaluated for its cost-effectiveness.
