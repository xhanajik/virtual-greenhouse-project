The DESIREE Project

webpage link

The DESIREE Project, funded by The European Union, started in 2016 and was completed in July 2019. One of Desiree’s many goals was to develop several tools focused on handling primary breast cancer. They designed and validated a working prototype of a decision support tool for the DESIREE platform [1].
More information about the platform, live demo availability, and a short demo video is posted on their website: http://www.desireeproject. eu/news/successful-completion-desiree-project.

Data
DESIREE implements a Breast Cancer Knowledge Model (BCKM). The BCKM leans on an Entity-Attribute-Value data model, with the main entities being the patient, the breast side, and the lesion [2]. This structure supports rule-based and subsumption-based reasoning to deliver three types of recommendations: guideline-based, case-based, and experience-based.

Approach
It has been noted that conforming to clinical guidelines gives breast cancer patients better overall survival rates and a greater chance of recurrence-free survival [3]. DESIREE GL-DSS (Guideline-based) represents selected clinical practice guidelines as IF-THEN rules and yields patient-specific recommendations, mainly in the form of procedures grouped into care plans [4]. By doing this, the DSS has the potential to improve clinician compliance with clinical guidelines and, therefore, continuously support physicians in choosing welldocumented and researched treatment plans.

On the contrary, for non-guideline-compliant decisions, DESIREE EXP-DSS (Experience-based) takes into account previous clinical outcomes to calculate recommendations’ confidence value (CV). New IF-THEN rules for the knowledge base are created by combining the patient characteristics, criteria for non-compliance to guidelines taken into consideration by the clinicians, and the final treatment that the MTB decided upon. The CV for new rules is set to 0 and gradually changes according to the positive or negative results. Treatment response outcomes, adverse events, and patient-reported outcomes (mainly in the form of questionnaires that reflect patients’ subjective satisfaction and quality of life) are all used metrics that adjust the CV [5].

The DESIREE CB-DSS (Case-based) depends on already solved cases, which are stored in the patient cases database. The DSS creates a query case and utilizes a similarity analyzer and retrieval algorithms to obtain the most similar patient cases. These cases can then be visualized using the rainbow box technique [4].

Usage
The DESIREE Project publish an official demo video [6], which demonstrated the platforms functionality well:

Clinicians have to manually add new patients into the system, and the patient files can be later checked and updated. The platform also supports adding medical image data into patient files. Other types of data, such as treatment information, adverse events associated with treatment, and patient-reported outcomes, are also managed in the system. Each patient file in the system contains tabs for the patient’s demographic data, history, first-visit information, radiodiagnosis, nuclear medicine data, surgery data, pathology data, oncology data, radiotherapy data, genetic data, follow-up, and patient-reported outcomes. The system includes tools to visualize certain patient data or statistics of treatments and outcomes. DESIREE can then produce a summary of the patient’s current status and evolution and a timeline of all past tests and treatments.

The platform is also useful in the instance of a Breast Unit meeting (essentially an MDT meeting for BC), where the system presents relevant information, imaging data, and similar cases. The system also implements guidelines and a conformance and evidence level, which can be selected as the final treatment. Nevertheless, a non-guideline form of treatment can also be entered into the system, along with the specific criteria for non-compliance to guidelines. Among those are also the following treatment-related patient restrictions: no surgery, no mastectomy, no endocrine therapy, no chemotherapy, monetary constraints, avoid delaying surgery, and distance constraints. Final decisions, with all of the criteria, are later utilized to suggest patient-specific recommendations in similar cases.

Advantages
The DESIREE platform provides a clean, unified interface to store many kinds of patient data and provide treatment suggestions. It also automatically adds to the patient records the final decisions of Breast Unit meetings and implements interactive visualization tools. The three types of recommendations offer a multitude of treatment options. The CDSS also considers some basic patient preferences, which gives the system a great advantage over other systems in this study.

Limitations
The online platform, while versatile, is not interconnected with any EHR system, so it will take health professionals considerable time to transfer patient data into the platform’s patient record.
